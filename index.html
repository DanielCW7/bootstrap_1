My journey as a programmer/web developer began in late 2019. I had graduated college with a degree in concrete industry management and had taken up an interest in computer science. In early 2019, I looked for ways I could utilize my artistic nature and my love of creating things in the realm of computers. I explored CAD design briefly. While it was interesting up to a point, it did not have the effect I was looking for exactly. So I thought about web design and ways I could get involved. My first idea was to join a coding bootcamp based out of Nashville that lasted three months, but it did not have the time commitment I had hoped for, so instead I opted for an online program for my education.

I got my start at Treehouse, an online resource where I could learn the fundamental skills needed to be a developer and web designer. I opted for the Front End Web Development techdegree they offered, and finished it in about 8 months. I studied for each project almost every day for a few hours at a time and dedicated the first half of each month to studying for a project by watching their modules. the latter half of the month was spent working on the project itself, which usually did not last more than 2 weeks at a time. I acquired my certification on August 4th, 2020. During my time spent studying under Treehouse, I came to learn about HTML, CSS, Javascript, SASS, APIs, Bootstrap, and more.

My goal is to find employment where I can put my skills to the test and see what I can do with a team of other like-minded individuals. My future plans after this are to learn new programming languages, frameworks, and someday put myself in a position where I can design landscapes and character models for companies like 343 Industries, Riot Games, and Activision!
